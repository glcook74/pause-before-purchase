How it works

A pause. A redirect. A better choice.

🛒

1. We spot the checkout

Dopamine Delay detects when you reach a checkout page on Amazon, ASOS, eBay, and 20+ other sites.

⏸

2. You get a moment

A gentle overlay asks: is this necessary, planned, or impulsive? No judgment — just awareness.

Labelling an impulse reduces its emotional intensity — affect labelling research, Lieberman et al. (2007)

🎯

3. You redirect or save

If it's impulsive, we offer something else for your brain to do — music, a puzzle, a breathing exercise. Or save it for later and earn Delay Points.

Offering an alternative dopamine source is evidence-based. Blocking without substituting creates resistance — Volkow et al. (2009)

Research shows that desire intensity for impulse purchases drops significantly within 24 hours. Our 'Save for Later' feature is built on exactly this finding.

Delay discounting literature, MacKillop et al. meta-analysis (2016)
